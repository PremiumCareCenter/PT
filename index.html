Common Physiotherapy Exercises

Understand the physics behind your recovery.

The Plank

**Physics:** Engages the core (Rectus Abdominis and Obliques) isometrically. The goal is to maintain a neutral spine against gravity, maximizing muscle time under tension for stability and endurance, not strength.

Knee Extension (Terminal)

**Physics:** Targets the VMO (Vastus Medialis Oblique) of the quadriceps. By focusing on the last 30 degrees of extension, it applies mechanical load specifically to improve tracking and stability around the patella (kneecap), critical for arthritis and post-op recovery.

Shoulder Scapular Squeezes

**Physics:** Activates the Rhomboids and middle Trapezius muscles. This exercise emphasizes retraction and depression of the scapula (shoulder blade) to improve posture and create a stable base for the rotator cuff, following the principle of proximal stability for distal mobility.
